Common Questions

AI Automations FAQ

What tools does Lumo use to build AI automations?

We work across n8n, Make (Integromat), Zapier, and custom Python pipelines depending on complexity and your existing stack. We always recommend the tool that offers the best balance of power, maintainability, and cost for your specific use case.

How long does it take to build an automation?

Simple single-system automations are delivered in 1–2 weeks. Complex multi-system pipelines with AI logic layers take 3–6 weeks. We always start with a discovery sprint to scope accurately before building.

Do I need a developer on my team to maintain the automations?

No. We build automations with maintainability in mind and provide full documentation. For n8n and Make workflows, non-technical team members can manage basic updates. We also offer ongoing maintenance retainers.

What kinds of processes can be automated?

Lead routing and CRM updates, outbound sequences, data enrichment, reporting pipelines, invoice processing, customer onboarding, internal approvals, social media scheduling, customer support triage, and much more. If a human is doing it on a screen, we can likely automate it.

How is AI Automation different from standard automation?

Standard automation follows rigid if/then logic. AI automation uses language models and classification models to handle unstructured data, make judgment calls, draft content, and process information that would normally require a human — making the automation far more powerful and flexible.
